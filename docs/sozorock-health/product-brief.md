# SozoRock Health Product Brief

## Product definition

SozoRock Health is a non-clinical health access intelligence and activation program of The SozoRock Foundation, Inc.

It helps underserved communities turn hidden access barriers into visible action through trusted access points, Voice Access, Health Access Day, Health Equity Hubs, provider-led pathways, workforce capacity, and county operating intelligence.

## Core promise

**Care for Every ZIP Code.**

## Positioning line

**SozoRock Health turns hidden access barriers into visible action for underserved communities.**

## Operating logic

**Signal → Decision → Action → Assurance → Impact**

- Signal: community access barriers, hub activity, ZIP-code gaps, digital readiness issues, travel burden, and local support patterns are identified.
- Decision: the system helps determine where attention is needed and what action should be prioritized.
- Action: teams can schedule Health Access Day events, activate hubs, assign digital access support, update local resources, or prepare county reports.
- Assurance: the model checks consent, confirms no protected health information is collected, preserves non-clinical boundaries, and requires human review before action.
- Impact: the system tracks access improvement, residents reached, hub engagement, workforce needs, and county-ready evidence.

## Product architecture

SozoRock Health has two connected layers.

### Resident Access Layer

Purpose: make access simple, private, and usable for residents.

Audience: underserved residents, families, caregivers, older adults, digitally excluded residents, transportation-limited households, and community members seeking support.

Experience standard: simple, clear, private, human.

Resident modules:

- Welcome
- Access Start
- Voice Access
- Health Access Day
- Health Equity Hubs
- Provider-Led Pathways

### County Operating Intelligence Layer

Purpose: turn access signals into decisions, actions, assurance, and measurable impact.

Audience: counties, funders, public-benefit stakeholders, internal teams, evaluators, and implementation partners.

Experience standard: geospatial, decision-driven, action-oriented, assurance-controlled.

County modules:

- Geospatial Access Operating Picture
- AI Decision Support
- Action Queue
- Assurance Log
- Scenario Planning

## Key program components

### Health Access Day

Health Access Day is the field activation model.

It brings health education, digital readiness, access support, and trusted engagement directly to underserved communities.

Its role is to identify access barriers, build trust, create resident engagement, generate non-clinical access signals, support county and funder evidence, and feed the County-Based Community Access Platform.

### Health Equity Hubs

Health Equity Hubs are the ongoing access channels.

They may include:

- library-based access
- community-based access
- home-based support for qualifying residents

Their purpose is to make health access support available beyond a one-day event.

### Voice Access

Voice Access is the AI-enabled resident support layer.

It helps residents speak or tap to find local support, locate Health Equity Hubs, prepare for Health Access Day, and understand non-clinical next steps.

Voice Access must not diagnose, treat, prescribe, triage, or give medical advice.

### County-Based Community Access Platform

The County-Based Community Access Platform is the system intelligence layer.

It turns local access signals into county-ready intelligence through maps, action queues, assurance controls, and scenario planning.

### Provider-Led Pathways

Provider-led pathways explain the Bring Your Own Platform model without confusing residents.

Resident-facing language:

**Providers keep their platforms. We help you get ready.**

Institutional meaning: licensed providers and health organizations retain responsibility for their own clinical systems, records, telehealth tools, compliance processes, medical judgment, and patient care. SozoRock Health supports access readiness and community activation without becoming the clinical provider.

### Workforce Capacity

SozoRock Health supports the non-clinical workforce needed to sustain access.

This includes:

- Digital Access Guides
- Community Access Teams
- Student Fellows
- Data and Insights Support
- Security Readiness Support
- AI Workflow Support
- Reporting and Evidence Support

## Data and trust standard

Locked trust language:

**No PHI. Consent-based. Non-clinical.**

This means SozoRock Health does not collect protected health information for the access model. It does not collect diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, medical records, clinical notes, or provider-patient data. It uses limited access signals such as ZIP code, hub interest, event interest, digital readiness needs, general support category, and follow-up permission. It requires consent for follow-up and keeps licensed care responsibility with providers.

## What SozoRock Health is not

SozoRock Health is not:

- a clinic
- a hospital
- a medical practice
- an insurer
- a telehealth provider
- a medical advice platform
- a clinical triage system
- a replacement for county health departments
- a replacement for licensed providers
- a generic resource directory
- a traditional referral agency
