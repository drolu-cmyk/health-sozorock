# SozoRock Health Brand System

## Brand owner

SozoRock Health is a program of The SozoRock Foundation, Inc.

## Design standard

The visual standard is the locked dual-layer mockup direction:

- Resident Access Layer: simple, clear, private, human.
- County Operating Intelligence Layer: geospatial, decision-driven, action-oriented, assurance-controlled.

## Primary message

**Care for Every ZIP Code.**

## Product positioning

**SozoRock Health turns hidden access barriers into visible action for underserved communities.**

## Operating line

**Residents get access. Counties get intelligence. Providers keep their platforms.**

## Provider pathway language

Resident-facing wording:

**Providers keep their platforms. We help you get ready.**

Institutional wording:

Licensed providers and health organizations retain responsibility for their own clinical systems, records, telehealth tools, compliance processes, medical judgment, and patient care. SozoRock Health supports access readiness and community activation without becoming the clinical provider.

## Trust language

Use:

**No PHI. Consent-based. Non-clinical.**

Do not use:

- privacy-minimal
- non-critical
- care navigator as the main role
- Sozo as assistant name

## Voice feature naming

Use:

**Voice Access**

Do not use:

- Sozo
- Sozo Assistant
- SozoBot
- Care Navigator

## Audience language

Use **underserved communities** as the primary audience language.

Use **rural** only as one access-barrier example when needed.

Approved audience examples:

- underserved residents
- older adults
- digitally excluded residents
- transportation-limited households
- low-income residents
- medically underserved communities
- rural residents as one segment, not the whole frame

## Visual direction

### Resident layer

- clean
- calm
- highly readable
- minimal text
- no photography
- no realistic human photos
- simple illustrations
- clear cards
- map and access-point cues where helpful

### County layer

- operating dashboard
- geospatial map
- action queue
- AI decision support
- assurance log
- scenario planning
- high contrast
- enterprise-grade typography
- clear status states
- action-focused cards

## Palette guidance

Use restrained colors:

- deep navy
- emerald green
- white
- soft blue-gray
- light neutral backgrounds
- restrained accent colors for status states

Avoid:

- loud gradients
- generic health-tech blue overload
- clinical hospital styling
- stock photography
- cartoon people-heavy visuals
- crowded dashboards

## Typography guidance

- use large, legible UI text
- avoid tiny unreadable labels in mockups
- keep headings short
- keep body copy plain
- prioritize clarity over decorative style

## Logo and brand assets

Expected asset paths:

- `public/brand/sozorock-health-logo.svg`
- `public/brand/sozorock-foundation-logo.svg`
- `public/brand/app-icon.svg`
- `public/brand/brand-mark.svg`

If these assets do not exist, do not recreate a logo without direction. Use text branding temporarily and report the missing files.

## Content tone

Use P&G-level clarity for resident-facing copy.

Use Palantir-style operating logic for the county-facing layer.

Avoid:

- hype
- vague AI language
- generic care navigation claims
- unsupported partnership claims
- clinical language
- medical promise language

## Locked visual principle

The app should not feel like a generic health app.

It should feel like a non-clinical access operating layer:

- residents see access
- counties see intelligence
- providers keep their platforms
